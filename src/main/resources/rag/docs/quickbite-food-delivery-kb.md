# QuickBite — Online Food Ordering & Delivery Knowledge Base (KB v1.0)

## 1) Business Overview

QuickBite is a food ordering and delivery platform connecting customers with restaurants and delivery partners.
This knowledge base defines customer-facing policies and standard support workflows.

Assistant goals:

- Provide clear next steps and expected timelines.
- Use policy-based answers only; do not guess.
- If required information is missing, ask a short clarifying question.
- Cite the relevant section title in every answer.

## 2) Key Terms

- Order Status: Created → Accepted → Preparing → Picked Up → Delivered (or Canceled)
- ETA: the estimated time of arrival shown in the app.
- Delivery Partner: the courier delivering the order.
- Merchant: the restaurant preparing the food.
- Service Credit: the non-cash credit applied to a future order.
- Refund: the return of funds to the original payment method.

## 3) Ordering Flow (What Customers Can Do)

Customers can:

1. Browse restaurants, menus, and availability windows.
2. Customize items (size, add-ons) and add notes (e.g., “no onions”).
3. Choose a delivery address and contact number.
4. Select a payment method and place the order.

Important:

- Special instructions are requests, not guarantees. Restaurants may be unable to fulfill some notes.
- Menu photos are illustrative; portion sizes may vary by restaurant.

## 4) Payments & Receipts

Supported payments:

- Card
- Wallet balance
- Gift voucher (if available)

Receipts:

- A digital receipt is available on the order details page.
- Receipts show itemized charges, delivery fee, service fee, discounts, and taxes (if applicable).

Payment troubleshooting:

- If payment failed, customers should retry once and confirm sufficient funds.
- If a charge appears twice, advise the customer to check pending authorizations; duplicate holds usually release within 3–7 business days.

## 5) Delivery Fees, Service Fees, and Tipping

- Delivery fees depend on distance, time of day, and demand.
- Service fees support platform operations and may vary by region.
- Tips (if enabled) go to the delivery partner. Tips are optional and typically non-refundable.

## 6) Cancellations (Customer-Initiated)

Cancellation rules depend on order status:

A) Before the restaurant accepts:

- Customers can cancel immediately in the app.
- If already charged, a refund is initiated automatically.

B) After the restaurant accepts but before pickup:

- Customer may request cancellation.
- If the restaurant has started preparing, the order may be non-refundable.

C) After pickup:

- Order cannot be canceled because preparation and pickup have occurred.
- Support should help with issues (late delivery, missing items, quality), not cancellation.

Support guidance:

- Ask for the order number and current status.
- Confirm whether the customer is trying to cancel due to a mistake, timing, or address issues.

## 7) Address Changes

Address changes are limited:

- If the order is not yet picked up, the customer can attempt to update the address in the app (if available).
- After pickup, address changes may not be possible.

If the customer entered the wrong address:

- Ask if they can meet the delivery partner at the original address.
- If not deliverable and the food cannot be rerouted, the order may be considered completed and non-refundable.

## 8) Late Delivery (ETA Issues)

Definition:

- An order is “late” if it is more than 15 minutes past the latest ETA shown in-app.

Standard workflow:

1. Confirm the ETA and current order status.
2. If “Preparing” for a long time:
	- Inform customer the restaurant is still preparing.
	- Offer to monitor for 10 minutes if the customer asks for an update.
3. If “Picked Up” and not moving:
	- Ask the customer to confirm the delivery address and phone number.
	- Attempt to contact the delivery partner (if your operations allow).
4. If late beyond 30 minutes past the latest ETA:
	- Offer a resolution based on the Late Delivery Resolution Matrix (Section 12).

Important:

- Avoid blaming the restaurant or courier. Use neutral language.
- Never promise exact arrival times; use estimates.

## 9) Missing Items, Wrong Items, and Order Accuracy

If the order is delivered but incorrect:

1. Ask what is missing/wrong and whether packaging seals were intact.
2. Request photos when possible:
	- Photo of received items
	- Photo of the receipt label on the bag (if present)

General rule:

- Restaurants are responsible for preparation accuracy.
- QuickBite provides refunds/credits according to the Resolution Matrix (Section 12).

If only a small add-on is missing:

- Prefer a partial refund or service credit.

## 10) Food Quality & Safety Complaints

Examples:

- Cold food
- Spilled items
- Damaged packaging
- Undercooked food
- Suspected contamination

Workflow:

1. Express concern and ask if anyone is feeling ill or in danger.
2. If immediate medical risk is mentioned: advise the customer to seek medical help and escalate internally.
3. Collect details:
	- Items affected
	- What was wrong
	- Photos (if available)
	- Time of delivery

Resolution:

- Use matrix in Section 12.
- For severe safety complaints, escalate for internal review.

## 11) Allergens and Dietary Restrictions (Important Disclaimer)

- Allergen information is provided by restaurants and may be incomplete.
- Cross-contamination can occur in restaurant kitchens.
- QuickBite cannot guarantee allergen-free preparation.

Support guidance:

- If a customer has a severe allergy, advise contacting the restaurant before ordering when possible.
- If an allergic reaction occurred, treat it as a safety escalation.

## 12) Resolution Matrix (Refunds & Credits)

Use these guidelines for customer support resolutions:

A) Late delivery (more than 30 minutes past the latest ETA)

- If delivered: offer 10–25% service credit depending on severity.
- If not delivered and the customer no longer wants the order: consider cancel and refund if possible.

B) Missing item(s)

- Refund the missing item(s) value or issue equivalent service credit.
- If the main item is missing (e.g., entrée): prefer refund to payment method.

C) Wrong item(s)

- Refund the wrong item(s) value or issue service credit.
- If the customer cannot eat the replacement due to dietary restriction: prefer refund.

D) Spillage or severely damaged items

- Refund affected items. If most of the order is affected, refund the full order.

E) Suspected food safety issue

- Escalate and consider refund of affected items or full order depending on severity.

Notes:

- Do not refund tips unless the policy explicitly allows it (tips are typically non-refundable).
- If the customer is repeatedly requesting refunds, escalate for account review.

## 13) Promotions, Coupons, and Price Differences

- Coupons apply only if entered before checkout and meet eligibility rules.
- Some promotions are restaurant-funded and cannot be applied after purchase.
- Menu prices may differ from in-store prices due to the platform and operational costs.

If a promotion didn’t apply:

- Confirm coupon code, expiration date, minimum spend, and eligible restaurants/items.
- If the promotion was valid but the system failed, issue a courtesy service credit.

## 14) Account & Privacy

- Customers can update phone number and address in profile settings.
- For security, do not disclose full payment details in chat.
- If account takeover is suspected, escalate and instruct the customer to reset the password immediately.

## 15) Escalation Triggers (Human / Ops Review)

Escalate if:

- Order marked delivered but the customer never received it.
- Suspected food safety incident with illness or injury.
- Repeated refund requests across multiple recent orders.
- Threats, harassment, or requests for legal documentation.
- Payment dispute / chargeback intent is stated.
